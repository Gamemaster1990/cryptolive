💡 Understanding Crypto Price Data

The prices shown on CryptoLive are volume-weighted averages aggregated from hundreds of cryptocurrency exchanges worldwide via CoinGecko. This means the price you see is not from a single exchange but represents the global average across all major trading platforms. Here is what each metric means:

Current Price
The latest volume-weighted average price across major exchanges, updated every 60 seconds.
24h Change (%)
Percentage price change over the last 24 hours. Green indicates an increase, red indicates a decrease.
Market Cap
Total value of all coins in circulation (price × circulating supply). Used to rank cryptocurrencies.
24h Volume
Total trading volume over the last 24 hours. Higher volume generally indicates higher liquidity and market activity.
24h High / Low
The highest and lowest prices recorded in the last 24 hours. Shows the daily trading range.
All-Time High (ATH)
The highest price a cryptocurrency has ever reached in its entire trading history.

🔍 How Crypto Prices Are Determined

Cryptocurrency prices, like any traded asset, are primarily determined by supply and demand dynamics on exchanges. When more people want to buy a cryptocurrency than sell it, the price rises. When selling pressure exceeds buying pressure, the price falls. However, several unique factors influence crypto prices:

  • Market Sentiment — News events, regulatory announcements, and social media sentiment can trigger rapid price movements.
  • Adoption & Utility — As more businesses and individuals use a cryptocurrency, demand typically increases.
  • Supply Mechanics — Some cryptocurrencies have fixed supplies (e.g., Bitcoin's 21 million cap), creating scarcity that can drive value.
  • Macroeconomic Factors — Inflation, interest rates, and global economic conditions increasingly influence crypto markets.
  • Technological Developments — Network upgrades, protocol changes, and new features can affect a coin's perceived value.
