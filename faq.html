What is cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security and operates on decentralized networks called blockchains. Unlike traditional currencies (like the US Dollar or Euro), cryptocurrencies are not issued or controlled by any central authority like a government or bank.

The first and most well-known cryptocurrency is Bitcoin, created in 2009 by an anonymous person or group using the name Satoshi Nakamoto. Today there are thousands of cryptocurrencies with various use cases — from digital cash (Bitcoin, Litecoin) to smart contract platforms (Ethereum, Solana) to decentralized finance applications.

How are cryptocurrency prices determined?

Cryptocurrency prices are primarily determined by supply and demand on exchanges — just like stocks, commodities, or any other traded asset. When more people want to buy a cryptocurrency than sell it, the price goes up. When more people want to sell, the price goes down.

Factors that influence crypto prices include:

  • Market sentiment — News, regulatory developments, and general public perception
  • Adoption — More businesses and individuals using a cryptocurrency increases demand
  • Technology upgrades — Network improvements or protocol changes can affect prices
  • Macroeconomic factors — Inflation, interest rates, and global economic conditions
  • Supply mechanics — Some coins have fixed supplies (like Bitcoin's 21 million cap), which affects scarcity

What is market capitalization?

Market capitalization (or "market cap") is the total value of all coins of a particular cryptocurrency in circulation. It's calculated as:

Market Cap = Current Price × Circulating Supply

Market cap is used to rank cryptocurrencies and gives you an idea of a coin's relative size and stability. Generally, coins with larger market caps (like Bitcoin and Ethereum) are considered more established and less volatile than smaller "altcoins."

Why are crypto prices so volatile?

Cryptocurrency markets are known for their volatility — prices can change by 5-10% or more in a single day. This happens for several reasons:

  • Market maturity — Crypto markets are still relatively young compared to stock markets
  • Liquidity — Some cryptocurrencies have lower trading volumes, making prices more sensitive to large trades
  • News sensitivity — Regulatory announcements, hacks, or celebrity tweets can cause rapid price swings
  • Speculation — Many traders focus on short-term price movements rather than long-term value
  • 24/7 trading — Unlike stock markets, crypto trades around the clock, including weekends and holidays

This volatility is why it's especially important to never invest more than you can afford to lose and to treat crypto prices on CryptoLive as informational only.

How fresh is the price data?

CryptoLive automatically refreshes price data every 60 seconds. However, there is always some inherent delay in the data pipeline — the CoinGecko API itself aggregates prices from exchanges, and there may be a delay of several seconds to a few minutes compared to real-time exchange prices.

For active trading, always use your exchange's real-time order book. CryptoLive is best for quick market checks and general awareness, not for making split-second trading decisions.

Are the prices 100% accurate?

We strive to provide accurate data, but prices displayed on CryptoLive should be considered indicative, not definitive. Small discrepancies may exist between the prices shown here and the actual prices on specific exchanges due to:

  • Different exchanges having slightly different prices (arbitrage)
  • Delays in data aggregation and API updates
  • Differences in trading pairs and liquidity across exchanges
