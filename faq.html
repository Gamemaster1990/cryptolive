📖 Basics of Cryptocurrency
What is cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security and operates on decentralized networks called blockchains. Unlike traditional currencies (like the US Dollar or Euro), cryptocurrencies are not issued or controlled by any central authority like a government or bank.

The first and most well-known cryptocurrency is Bitcoin, created in 2009 by an anonymous person or group using the name Satoshi Nakamoto. Today there are thousands of cryptocurrencies with various use cases — from digital cash (Bitcoin, Litecoin) to smart contract platforms (Ethereum, Solana) to decentralized finance applications. For a more in-depth explanation, read our complete beginner's guide to cryptocurrency.

How are cryptocurrency prices determined?

Cryptocurrency prices are primarily determined by supply and demand on exchanges — just like stocks, commodities, or any other traded asset. When more people want to buy a cryptocurrency than sell it, the price goes up. When more people want to sell, the price goes down.

Factors that influence crypto prices include market sentiment, adoption rates, technology upgrades, macroeconomic factors, and supply mechanics. CryptoLive aggregates price data from major exchanges via the CoinGecko API to show you a volume-weighted average price.

What is market capitalization?

Market capitalization (or "market cap") is the total value of all coins of a particular cryptocurrency in circulation. It is calculated as: Market Cap = Current Price × Circulating Supply.

Market cap is used to rank cryptocurrencies and gives you an idea of a coin's relative size and stability. Generally, coins with larger market caps (like Bitcoin and Ethereum) are considered more established and less volatile than smaller "altcoins." For a detailed explanation, see our guide to market capitalization.

Why are crypto prices so volatile?

Cryptocurrency markets are known for their volatility — prices can change by 5-10% or more in a single day. This happens because crypto markets are still relatively young, some coins have lower liquidity making prices sensitive to large trades, the market is highly sensitive to news and regulatory announcements, many traders focus on short-term speculation, and crypto trades 24/7 including weekends and holidays.

Always verify critical price information on your exchange before making trading decisions.

What does 24h volume mean?

24h volume is the total amount of a cryptocurrency traded across all exchanges in the last 24 hours. It is an important metric because higher trading volume generally indicates higher liquidity — meaning you can buy or sell larger amounts without significantly affecting the price. Low volume coins may experience more price slippage when trading. On CryptoLive, you can see individual coin volumes in the expanded detail view and aggregate volume in the stats bar at the top.

📚 Blockchain & Crypto Education
What is blockchain technology?

Blockchain is the underlying technology behind most cryptocurrencies. It is a distributed, decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across many computers. Each "block" contains a group of transactions, and blocks are linked together in a "chain." Once recorded, data in a block cannot be altered retroactively without changing all subsequent blocks — which would require consensus from the majority of the network. This makes blockchain immutable and transparent. Learn more in our complete blockchain explainer.

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin (BTC) is the world's first cryptocurrency, created in 2009 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto. It introduced the concept of a decentralized digital currency that operates without any central authority. Bitcoin uses proof-of-work consensus and has a fixed supply cap of 21 million coins, making it a deflationary asset. Often called "digital gold," Bitcoin is primarily used as a store of value and a hedge against inflation. It has the largest market cap and strongest brand recognition of any cryptocurrency.

What is the difference between Bitcoin and Ethereum?

While both are leading cryptocurrencies, they serve fundamentally different purposes. Bitcoin is designed as a decentralized digital currency and store of value — it excels at being a secure, predictable monetary system. Ethereum is a smart contract platform that enables developers to build decentralized applications (dApps) on its blockchain. Ethereum's native currency, Ether (ETH), is used to pay for computation on the network. Ethereum transitioned from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake in 2022 (The Merge), reducing its energy consumption by ~99.9%. See our Top Cryptocurrencies page for more details on both.

What is a stablecoin?

A stablecoin is a type of cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value by being pegged to a real-world asset, most commonly the US Dollar. Examples include Tether (USDT) and USD Coin (USDC). Stablecoins play a crucial role in the crypto ecosystem by providing a stable medium of exchange and a safe haven during market volatility. Traders use them to move funds between exchanges without converting to traditional fiat currency, and they are widely used in DeFi applications for lending and borrowing.

How do I choose which cryptocurrency to invest in?

When evaluating cryptocurrencies, consider these factors: market cap ranking (larger cap coins are generally more established), the project's technology and use case (what problem does it solve?), the development team (are they transparent and experienced?), community activity (is there genuine interest and development?), trading volume and liquidity, and the project's roadmap and milestones. Remember that this is not financial advice — always do your own research (DYOR) and never invest more than you can afford to lose. See our beginner's guide for more context.

What is DeFi (Decentralized Finance)?

DeFi refers to financial applications built on blockchain technology that operate without intermediaries like banks, brokerages, or insurance companies. DeFi platforms enable lending, borrowing, trading, and earning interest on crypto assets through smart contracts. Most DeFi applications run on Ethereum, Solana, and other smart contract platforms. While DeFi offers exciting opportunities for financial inclusion and innovation, it also comes with risks including smart contract bugs, hacks, and impermanent loss in liquidity pools.

What is Bitcoin Dominance?

Bitcoin Dominance is Bitcoin's market cap as a percentage of the total cryptocurrency market cap. For example, if Bitcoin's market cap is $1 trillion and the total crypto market cap is $2 trillion, Bitcoin dominance is 50%. When Bitcoin dominance is high (50-70%), it suggests investors prefer Bitcoin over altcoins. When it is low (under 40%), it indicates an "altcoin season" where alternative cryptocurrencies are gaining market share. While CryptoLive does not currently display Bitcoin dominance directly, you can infer it by comparing Bitcoin's individual market cap to the total market cap shown at the top of the page.

🛡️ Security & Safety
How do I stay safe from crypto scams?

Cryptocurrency security is essential. Follow these best practices: never share your private keys or seed phrase with anyone; use reputable exchanges with a proven track record; enable two-factor authentication (2FA) using an authenticator app (not SMS); be skeptical of unsolicited investment offers and "guaranteed returns"; always verify URLs before connecting your wallet to any website; use a hardware wallet for long-term storage of significant amounts; and send a small test transaction before transferring large amounts. For a comprehensive guide, read our crypto security tips.

What is a hardware wallet?

A hardware wallet is a physical device that stores your cryptocurrency private keys offline, protecting them from online hacking attempts. Popular options include Ledger and Trezor. Hardware wallets are considered the most secure way to store cryptocurrency because your private keys never touch the internet. When you need to make a transaction, you confirm it on the device itself. For any significant amount of cryptocurrency you plan to hold long-term, a hardware wallet is strongly recommended. Even if your computer is infected with malware, your funds remain safe in a hardware wallet.
